How to Give through the Tin Cup Challenge

During the Giving Period each summer, you can give as a community donor or a Challenger. Com­mu­ni­ty donors make a sin­gle des­ig­nat­ed gift, with their cho­sen amounts allo­cat­ed to spe­cif­ic non­prof­its they wish to sup­port. Chal­lengers and Friends of the Challenge pro­vide undes­ig­nat­ed fund­ing to par­tial­ly match the des­ig­nat­ed gifts of com­mu­ni­ty donors, sub­stan­tial­ly increas­ing the impact of each par­tic­i­pat­ing organization’s fundrais­ing efforts.

As a Tin Cup Donor, making direct designated gifts:

  1. 100% of your donation goes to the nonprofits of your choice.
  2. Provide funding to promote and support the charitable mission of your organizations.
  3. Simplify giving by making a single payment to the Community Foundation of Teton Valley on behalf of local nonprofits.

Both ways of giving are intrinsic to our philanthropic model.

Challengers are a special group of businesses and individuals who give $1,500 or more to the Challenger Fund annually. Challengers are community leaders who promote charitable giving and intrinsically value Teton Valley’s nonprofits. The generosity of our Challengers inspires increased community giving and is the key to the Tin Cup Challenge’s success.

Gifts to the Challenger Fund are used to directly benefit local nonprofits by:

  • Providing a partial Match Grant for participating nonprofits for funds raised during the Giving Period.
  • Covering costs associated with the 6-week Giving Period and Event Day, ensuring nonprofits receive 100% of their designated donations.
  • Funding the Community Foundation of Teton Valley’s Competitive Grant Program, Youth Philanthropy, and Nonprofit Workshops for board and staff.
